Manning's Equation

Manning's equation is used extensively in the field of water resources. Manning's equation is used to calculate the total flow through an open channel given a set of parameters. An example of open channel flow is illustrated below. One very useful adaptation of the Manning's Equation can be used to find the mean velocity of the water in the channel. The equation to find the velocity using Mannings equation is shown below. The variables needed to calculate the velocity are also defined below.

Solving for Velocity Using Manning's Equation

v = (kn / n) Rh^(2/3) S^(1/2)

where

v = cross-sectional mean velocity (ft/s, m/s)

kn = 1.486 for English units and kn = 1.0 for SI units

n = Manning coefficient of roughness

Rh = hydraulic radius (ft, m)

S = slope - or gradient - of pipe (ft/ft, m/m)
